
    011 Drowning in a sea of Chinese characters  淹没在汉字的海洋里
    
    What the writer want to express
    The writer's original version of Chinese
    
    我的邻居汉语学了两年了。 
    她说，她感到气馁了，学习汉语是不可能完成的任务。
    我就告诉她，一步一个脚印，她看太远了。
    她说她觉得在一片汉字海中自己正在淹没。
    我就建议她找一本她喜欢看的书，为了感觉好阅读。
    我不认为改变了她的主意，她还显得很沮丧。
    
    My correction
    
    我的邻居汉语学了两年了(或者 我的邻居学了两年汉语了)
    她说，她感到气馁了，学会汉语是不可能完成的任务。
    我就告诉她，(学习汉语)要一步一个脚印，她太贪求了.
    她说她觉得自己好像正淹没在一片汉字海中。
    我就建议她找一本她喜欢看的书，读一读, (从书中)找点乐子.
    我觉得没能改变她的想法，因为她还是显得很沮丧。
    
    The writer's native language version
    
    My neighbour has studied Chinese for two years.
    She said that she felt discouraged, that learning Chinese is an impossible task.
    I told her that she should just take one step at a time, that she is looking too far ahead.
    She said that she felt she is drowning in a sea of Chinese characters.
    I advised her to find a book she likes and read it for pleasure.
    I don't think I changed her mind since/as/beacause she still looked pretty discouraged.
    
    Comparison of the two languages  中英文对照.
    
    我的邻居汉语学了两年了(或者 我的邻居学了两年汉语了)
    My neighbour has studied Chinese for two years.

    I told her that she should just take one step at a time, she is looking too for ahead.
    
    她说她觉得自己好像正淹没在一片汉字海中。
    She said she felt she was drowning in a sea of Chinese characters.
    
    我就建议她找一本她喜欢看的书，读一读, (从书中)找点乐子.
    I advised her to find a book she likes and read it for pleasure.
    
    我觉得没能改变她的想法，因为她还是显得很沮丧。
    I don't think I changed her mind since/as/beacause she still looked pretty discouraged.
